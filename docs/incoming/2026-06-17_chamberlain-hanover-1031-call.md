# §1031 Counsel Call — Chamberlain + Kao + Hanover

**Date:** June 17, 2026
**Status:** COUNSEL (first outside §1031 review) — preliminary discussion, **not** a written opinion. Pending confirmation with Hanover's outside tax counsel.

## Participants (as stated on the call)

## Key takeaways (summary — see verbatim transcript below)

- **Bargain sale to a charity.** Belara appraised at **~$28M** (March 2026 appraisal) but is being sold to **Strake Jesuit for $20M** — a bargain sale to a school generating **~$8M of charitable deduction**. Strake is land-constrained and wants the parcel for parking next to its stadium.
- **Replacement benchmark ~$28M (counsel's read), not $20M.** Counsel called this a "clash"/"dysfunction" of tax-code sections: the transaction is deemed ~$28M of value for §1031 even though the price is $20M. Full deferral would need ~$28M of qualifying replacement in place by Day 180 — not achievable on this build.
- **What counts by Day 180:**
  - **Land (~$6.5M): yes.**
  - **Site work (clearing, grading, moving dirt, some utilities): probably not** — counsel's view is a qualifying improvement must be physically completed and attached to the land; site work likely is not, though it is in tax basis and arguable.
  - **Soft costs (architecture, engineering, permits, zoning, environmental): no** — treated as "fees," not improvements.
- **Base case:** defer only the gain attributable to the **$6.5M land** (~a quarter of the $28M). Roughly framed as ~$6M of gain sheltered; dollar tax benefit ballparked on the call (~$0.75M–$1.2M) but **not pinned down** (Hobbs: "I'm not sure the gain is $6 million").
- **Upside case:** try to count some **site work**. Counsel leans against it, but disallowance likely kills **only that portion**, not the whole exchange — so low downside to trying. (Ethan: "if it doesn't [disqualify the whole thing], then we'd go for it.")
- **Recommended structure — no "drop."** Belara's owner forms a **disregarded single-member LLC (SMLLC)**; the SMLLC buys the land with QI proceeds. Because it is disregarded, Belara is treated as the buyer for tax — **no drop occurs**. Hanover invests into that entity **later**, converting it to a partnership. Lower IRS-risk profile than a classic drop-and-swap. In the base case (conceding site work) **no EAT is needed** — the QI simply holds proceeds to buy the land.
- **Cash sequencing — do not pay tax first.** Route the $20M through the **QI**, deploy ~$6.5M into the land, then take the remainder back from the QI and pay tax on it. Paying tax outright first blows the exchange. Keep-vs-pay decision on the remainder expected within **~30–45 days** of closing.
- **Intent-to-sell risk.** Plan is to lease up and sell the industrial building — cuts against §1031 (inventory risk). Mitigate: do not document an intent to sell; frame a future sale as a major decision requiring both partners' approval, with a buy-sell. Hobbs sized the risk at **~1-in-15 to 1-in-20**. If the IRS nullified it: owe deferred tax + interest; **penalty likely waived** (good-faith position). Not a current IRS audit-focus area. **Signing leases helps** (evidences holding for investment).
- **Construction / timing.** Industrial land close scheduled **Sept 15, 2026** (Day 1). Construction contract **~$40M total, ~$11M site work**. First-180-day spend estimated **$8–11M**, mostly site work. Permit delays of 60–90 days could shrink the site-work window (might only get ~4 months).
- **Assignment.** Hanover will assign the land PSA to the new entity; need to confirm it can be assigned without the seller's consent (the entity won't be a Hanover affiliate until Hanover enters later).
- **Status.** Hanover is willing to accommodate the structure. Next: a call with **Jeff Wallace** (Hanover's outside tax counsel) to confirm, then the family decides.
