The gap between "money deployed" and "value the IRS credits" is where skepticism belongs: only improvements **physically in place**, **paid with exchange funds** (via QI/EAT), and **documented** at Day 180 count. Slippage on permits, land close, EAT formation, or draw certification could turn a comfortable $1.5M cushion into boot.

## 3. Construction feasibility vs. IRS counting rules

These are **different questions**. Conflating them is how families get surprised at Day 180.

### A. Construction feasibility (can the job move fast enough?)

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| 180 days ≈ 6 months vs. ~11-month full build | ~55% of schedule — sitework + foundations + early vertical is standard front-loaded scope |
| Term sheet gate | Simultaneous close requires **land disturbance permit** before construction start — permit timing is on the critical path |
| Hanover as GC | Affiliate GC (4.5% GMP); term sheet requires GMP at close — feasible if docs ready, not automatic |
| Exchange equity first | Matches how partners want capital stack drawn; avoids boot from under-deployment of proceeds |

**Verdict:** Construction **can** hit the user's pace — **if** permit, EAT, land PSA, and lender draw procedures are aligned by Day 0. That is a **coordination** bet, not physics.

### B. IRS / tax counting (what counts toward ~$20M?)

Per unreviewed lawyer primer + DIGEST + Rev. Proc. 2000-37 discipline (general — **NEEDS COUNSEL**):

| Item | Counts toward exchange value? |
|---|---|
| Land acquired by EAT, paid with QI funds | **Yes** |
| Improvements **physically in place** on Day 180 | **Yes** — to extent completed |
| Improvements **paid for** with exchange funds (QI → EAT → contractor) | **Yes** — payment path matters |
| **Documented** in-place value (cost records, GC certs, Day-180 valuation) | **Required** to defend amount — not automatic |
| Sitework/foundations **complete** but only **billed**, not paid from exchange | **Risk** — payment + in-place both matter |
| Materials **stored off-site** | **No** (per DIGEST / Structure A drafts) |
| Deposits, prepaids, soft costs without in-place improvement | **No** / **uncertain** |
| Dev fee (4%), GC fee (4.5%), equity funding fee | **Generally not** replacement property — can erode deployable exchange dollars |
| Work completed **after** EAT → exchanger deed | **No** for exchange — funds post-transfer are post-exchange |
| Construction loan draws | **Debt is fine** for §1031 — but loan proceeds used for improvements still need to trace to **exchange-qualified** spend during parking |

### C. The three-part test (working shorthand)

For each dollar toward the ~$20M:

1. **In place** — physically part of the replacement property on Day 180  
2. **Paid** — exchange proceeds (or exchange-titled debt policy per counsel) flowed through QI/EAT  
3. **Documented** — draw records, lien waivers, GC % complete, independent Day-180 valuation  

**Boot math:** Belara ~$20M, no debt → full ~$20M must be reinvested in qualifying replacement property. Shortfall = taxable boot (§1031(b)). User's $21.5M model − ~$1.5M fees/contingency slippage − documentation haircut = still likely above $20M **if execution is clean** — but "clean" is the hard part.

- [ ] GC schedule of values / % complete tied to **in-place** work

## 6. Risks if Belara close and land close diverge

The model assumes **simultaneous alignment** (Sept 1). Real deals rarely stay pinned.

### Scenario matrix

| Scenario | Clock start | Land / EAT | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Belara Aug 20, land Sept 1** | Aug 20 | Land closes **12 days after** clock starts | Day 180 → **~Feb 16, 2027** — **loses ~12 days** of construction window; identification due **~Oct 4** |
| **Belara Sept 1, land Sept 15** | Sept 1 | Land delayed 2 weeks | Exchange clock running **without** replacement land in EAT — QI holds cash; may need reverse-exchange parking discipline or delayed land acquisition; **45-day ID still fires** |
| **Belara Oct 1, land Sept 1** | Oct 1 | Land closed **before** Belara | **Serious:** acquiring replacement before relinquished sale closes is **reverse-exchange** territory — different docs, EAT must hold land **before** Belara proceeds exist; not impossible but **not** the current simple forward model |
| **Belara slips 30+ days, land fixed Sept 1** | Later | Land Sept 1 anyway | Worst case: land bought with non-exchange funds or wrong entity, then Belara closes — **step-transaction / funding trace** risk; may need rescission or restructure |
| **Belara closes, land PSA fails** | Belara date | No replacement | 45-day ID must identify property **actually obtainable**; backup DST critical; boot if nothing acquired by Day 180 |

### Top risks (ranked)

1. **Clock starts on Belara, not land** — Every day of Belara delay compresses the improvement window. User model assumes ~5 months of improvement draws **after** Day 0 land; late Belara with fixed land close does **not** extend Day 180.

2. **Entity mismatch** — Belara sold by **Titan Management**; term sheet LP is **Kao Management Trust**. If land/EAT title is in the wrong entity, identification and deed at Day 180 may not complete a valid exchange despite correct dates.

3. **Forward vs. reverse confusion** — If NFCC land must close before Belara (seller pressure, PSA dates), the transaction is structurally harder than the lawyer primer's simple forward timeline. Requires EAT to park land **first**, with Belara as identified relinquished property within 45 days of EAT acquisition (Rev. Proc. 2000-37 timing — **NEEDS COUNSEL**).

4. **Term sheet simultaneous close** — Hanover template: JV + GMP + loan + land + permit **same day**. That day must be **Day 0 or shortly after Belara**, not before; and JV membership at close **breaks §1031** unless restructured (EAT/TIC/Structure A).

5. **Permit lag** — Term sheet requires land disturbance permit at close. If permit slips but land closes, exchange funds sit in EAT on land only ($6.5M) — boot risk grows unless improvement spend accelerates.

6. **Documentation lag** — February draws for "foundations" may be **paid** in February but **certified in-place** after Day 180 if inspections slip — taxable boot on paper even if concrete is poured.

### Mitigations (planning, not advice)

- **Single master calendar** — Belara PSA, land PSA, permit, EAT, 45-day, 180-day on one timeline  
- **Pick one Day 0** — Belara drives clocks; land close ≤ Day 0 or within days, with EAT on title first if reverse  
- **Resolve Titan vs. Kao Trust** before any PSA  
- **Backup DST** in 45-day letter  
- **Extend 2026 return**  
- **Day-180 valuation** commissioned in advance, not after the fact
